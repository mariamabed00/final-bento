Hidden allergens
and where they may be present

Labels that may indicate the presence of egg protein

Albumin Globulin Ovamucoid
Binder Lecithin Ovovitellin
Coagulant Livetin Lysozyme
Vitellin Ovalbumin Emulsifier

Food that commonly contain egg protein

Most baked goods Baking mixes Marshmallows
Baking mixes Breakfast cereals Cake flours
Creamy fillings Processed meats (eg. sausages) Some salad dressings
Soups Hollandaise sauce Custard



*Egg protiens could also be found in cosmetics, shampoos and pharmaceuticals.
People allergic to eggs should also be careful when buying fried foods from vendors as they may be using the same frying surface to prepare multiple types of foods.

Labels that may indicate the presence of milk protein

Artificial butter flavor Butter Buttermilk solids
Caramel flavoring or color Solids Lactalbmin
Cream Whey High protein flavor
Sour cream Curds Rennet casein

Food that commonly contain milk protein

Batter fried foods Baked goods Packaged soups
Pies Custard Sausages
Soy cheese Cream sauces Sherbet
Cream soups Gravy Instant mashed potatoes

Labels that may indicate the presence of soy protein

Gum arabic Bulking agent Miso
Textured vegetable protein Strach Tufo
Protein extender Vegetable broth Vegetable starch
Soy sauce Lecithin Guar gum

Food that commonly contain soy protein

Baby food Chinese foods Hotdogs
Burger patties Canned soups Powdered meal replacers
Artificial cheese Stock cubes Some sauces (ex. Worcestershire)
Snack bars Shortenings Cooking oils

*Soybean flour is often added to cereal flour and is used extensively in the baking industry. The majority of breads contain some soy flour. Pastries, cakes, biscuits, and baby foods may contain soy flour.

Labels that may indicate the presence of wheat protein

Flour Strach Protein
Vegetable gum Bulgur Monosodium glutamate
Cornstrach Gluten Kamut
Farina Semolina Spelt

Food that commonly contain wheat protein

Gravy Luncheon products Malt
Macaroni Malted milks Processed meats
Soup mixes Soy sauce Cereal
Snack foods Chocolate candy Crackers, etc.

*Buckwheat is not a member of the grass family and is thus not a true cereal.

Labels that may indicate the presence of peanut protein

Peanut butter Emulsifier Oriental sauces

Food that commonly contain peanut or peanut oil

Margarine Marzipan Milk formula
Biscuits Satay Chili
Chinese dishes Vegetable oil Thai dishes
Pastry Cereal-based products Some soups

*Soybean flour is often added to cereal flour and is used extensively in the baking industry. The majority of breads contain some soy flour. Pastries, cakes, biscuits, and baby foods may contain soy flour.
